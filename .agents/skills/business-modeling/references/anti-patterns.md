# Business Modeling — Anti-Patterns

Load in `business-modeling` Step 5. For each fire: rewrite the box OR mark `[HYPOTHESIS — needs validation]` with a named test.

## Segment box

- **"Everyone" / "consumers" / "businesses" / "developers"** — reject. Push to a specific persona in a specific situation.
- **Two segments stuffed into one box** — split into two canvases or pick the primary one and mark the other as future.
- **B2B segments without a buyer named** — for enterprise/mid-market: name role + budget + procurement path.

## Channels box

- **"SEO" / "content marketing" / "social media" / "ads" / "virality"** without specifying — flag. Force the wedge.
- **Multiple channels listed equally** — early-stage businesses have ONE channel that works first. Pick the wedge; everything else is deprioritised.
- **Sales-led without a named ICP-to-rep ratio** — flag.

## Customer Relationships box (BMC)

- **"Self-service"** without an onboarding mechanic — flag. Self-service requires designed activation, not just docs.
- **"Personal assistance"** without a CS/sales motion budgeted — flag. CS costs money.

## Value Proposition / UVP box

- **"Increase X" / "Improve Y" / "Make Z better"** without a metric and baseline — rewrite as falsifiable claim.
- **"World-class" / "seamless" / "next-generation" / "AI-powered"** — strip marketing language; reduce to verifiable specifics.
- **The UVP is a feature list** — the UVP is the outcome; the features go in the Solution box.

## Solution box (Lean Canvas)

- **More than 3 features** — force prioritisation. The top-3 are the testable solution.
- **"AI-powered" as a feature** — AI is a tool; the feature is what the AI does for the user.

## Revenue / Monetisation box

- **"Freemium"** without a conversion mechanic — name where users hit the paywall and why they pay.
- **"We'll figure it out"** — KILL flag for ideas where viability depends on revenue.
- **No pricing number** — even a wrong number is better than no number; force a hypothesis with `[HYPOTHESIS]`.
- **TAM / market-size language in revenue box** — revenue is per-customer; market size belongs to `idea-evaluation`.

## Cost Structure box

- **No order-of-magnitude numbers** — call `fermi` to estimate. Even a range beats a label.
- **"Lean operations"** without specifics — name the dominant cost category and approximate $.

## Key Resources / Activities / Partnerships (BMC)

- **"Great team"** in Key Resources — flag. Specific named skills only.
- **"Will partner with X"** without contact — mark `[HYPOTHESIS]`.
- **AWS / Stripe / OpenAI listed as Key Partners** — these are vendors, not strategic partnerships. Reclassify.

## Unfair Advantage / Moat box

This is the most-fluffed box. Hard rejects:
- **"AI"** — AI is a capability available to everyone with an OpenAI key.
- **"Network effects"** without a bootstrap path — almost no early-stage business has network effects yet.
- **"Community"** without a named community asset (Discord with N members, owned newsletter with N subs).
- **"Data"** without specifying what data + how it accumulates + why competitors can't replicate.
- **"First-mover"** — almost never durable on its own.
- **"Founder team"** — necessary but not differentiating for the model.

Acceptable: proprietary dataset (named), exclusive partnership (signed), regulatory licence (held), switching-cost mechanism (specific), founder credibility/access (named credentials), distribution lock-in (specific), brand asset (measurable).

## Process anti-patterns

- **All boxes filled with full prose** — boxes should be tight bullets. Length = obscuring weakness.
- **Top 3 assumptions are not falsifiable** — every assumption needs a measurable threshold.
- **The canvas is treated as evidence** — a canvas is a hypothesis. Filling it perfectly is not validation. Always end with the next test.
