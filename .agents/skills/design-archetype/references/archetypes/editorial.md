# Archetype: editorial

**Feels like:** Substack, Medium 2014, NYT, Stripe Press, Are.na, The Verge (newsroom), Pitchfork

For: content-led products where reading IS the experience. Long-form, structured prose, attention to typography and rhythm.

## Typography
- Display: Editorial serif — Tiempos Headline, GT Sectra, Recoleta, Canela, ABC Diatype Mono Edu, Domaine Display, Source Serif Pro. 600–800 weight at 32–80px.
- Body: Highly readable serif (Tiempos Text, Source Serif, Lora, Iowan Old Style) OR confident grotesque (Söhne, Inter at 17–19px). Line-height 1.6–1.8.
- Mono: optional for code blocks or marginalia.
- Pairing rationale: explicit display/text pairing — a serif display with a serif text, OR a serif display with a grotesque body. The pairing carries the editorial voice.

## Color
- Background: paper tones — `#FAFAF7`, `#F5F2EC`, `#FFFAF0`. Off-white is mandatory.
- Foreground: rich black `#1A1A1A`–`#232323`. High contrast for reading.
- Accent: usually a single editorial accent — a deep red, a forest green, a warm amber. Used for links, pull-quotes, marginalia.
- Anti-defaults: NO neutral greys (dead). NO modern multi-accent palettes. NO pure white backgrounds for long-form reading.

## Motion
- Duration budget: 200–400ms. Motion is rare; reading is still.
- Easing: classical curves, nothing springy.
- Character: quiet, almost absent. Motion should not interrupt reading.

## Density
**Generous.** Reading-optimized line lengths (60–75ch for body). Margins and white space serve the rhythm.

## Icon stance
**hand-drawn** or sparse decorative use. Icons are rare; marginalia and dingbats more common. If functional icons needed, custom or Phosphor Thin at small sizes, NEVER prominent.

## Layout signatures
- Single column for body text, max-width 620–720px
- Pull-quotes that break the column rhythm
- Drop caps on opening paragraphs
- Marginalia / sidenotes (Tufte-style) for context
- Hairline rules between sections, not heavy borders
- Photography full-bleed or precisely cropped to the column
- Section breaks marked with a glyph or asterism (• • •)
- Bylines, dates, reading time treated with typographic care

## Reference sites (visit these)
1. **press.stripe.com** — editorial as product, type-set with care
2. **www.are.na/about** — editorial minimalism, generous type
3. **substack.com** (a well-designed publication, not the platform shell) — long-form reading flow

## Anti-patterns (do NOT do these)
- Card-based content lists (kills reading flow)
- Multi-column text on screen (newspaper instinct that fails on web)
- Decorative icons next to headings
- Sticky social-share rails that crowd the reading area
- Auto-playing video
- Dark mode as inverted lightness (must be a separate paper-and-ink color story)
- Sans-serif body for long-form
