# Archetype: brutalist-distinctive

**Feels like:** Gumroad, Posthog, Vercel labs, Cron 2022, Are.na, Bandcamp, Tonal, certain Read.cv cards

For: products that win by being unapologetically themselves. Strong identity beats polish. Ugly-on-purpose is acceptable when it's a position.

## Typography
- Display: Mono families, slab serifs, or unfashionable grotesques used confidently — JetBrains Mono Display, Söhne Mono, Berkeley Mono, ABC Diatype, Trickster, GT Pressura, IBM Plex Mono.
- Body: matched mono OR a deliberately plain humanist (Charter, Iowan, Inter at small sizes).
- Pairing rationale: the type choice IS the differentiation. A mono display says "we're for builders". A slab says "we're craft". The choice must be defended.

## Color
- Background: stark — pure white OR pure black is acceptable here (the ONLY archetype where this is OK). Or a single saturated brand color taking over the page.
- Foreground: high contrast.
- Accent: maximalist or absent. Often ONE giant color used as a wash (Gumroad pink, Posthog orange) OR full multi-color riso-print palette.
- Anti-defaults: NO Tailwind defaults, NO subtle palette work, NO "tasteful" neutrals — that misses the point.

## Motion
- Duration budget: 200–600ms with character or near-zero (snappy, mechanical).
- Easing: stepped, linear, or unusual curves. NOT smooth.
- Character: deliberate, confident, weird where needed.

## Density
**Variable.** Often dense like a print zine, sometimes wildly generous. The choice signals position — there is no neutral default.

## Icon stance
**hand-drawn** or **mixed-metaphor** — emoji used unironically, hand-drawn marks, ASCII boxes, system glyphs (✶ ✻ → ←). Stock icon libraries sit poorly here.

## Layout signatures
- Large blocks of solid color
- Type-as-image (display sized to fill the viewport)
- Visible grid (12-column with hairlines exposed)
- ASCII-style decorative elements
- Loud, confident headlines
- Asymmetric, sometimes slightly off-grid placements
- "Documentation as art" (Posthog feel)
- Borders that are 2px solid black instead of subtle hairlines

## Reference sites (visit these)
1. **gumroad.com** — pink wash, type confidence, deliberately not-Linear
2. **posthog.com** — hedgehog mascot + brutalist marks + dense product info
3. **are.na** — restraint version of the same instinct (brutal in its quietness)

## Anti-patterns (do NOT do these)
- Picking this archetype because you can't decide on another (cardinal sin)
- Half-committing — soft brutalism reads as just sloppy
- Gradients (kills the position)
- Tasteful drop shadows
- Subtle anything
- Apologizing for the boldness with rounded corners and soft type

## When NOT to use
- B2B with conservative buyers
- Health, finance, legal — trust collapses
- Products where users care about polish more than personality
- Anywhere brand identity isn't already this confident
