# Idea Evaluation — Anti-Patterns

Load in `idea-evaluation` Step 7. For any anti-pattern that fires, mark the affected dimension `flagged` and force user response before allowing GO.

## Segment / desirability

- **"Everyone is the customer"** — segment cannot be "consumers", "businesses", "developers", "creators". Push to a specific persona in a specific situation.
- **Friend/family ICP** — claims of demand validated by friends/family count as zero unless those people are independently in the target segment.
- **Solution-first framing** — "an app that uses AI to help X" with no description of the painful workaround = no pain identified yet.

## Why now / market

- **Vague why-now** — "AI is changing things", "the world is moving online", "post-COVID behaviour" without a specific shift in the last 24 months.
- **TAM = global market size** — "the freelance economy is $1.5T" is not a market for this product. Use SOM (year-1 reachable revenue) only.
- **"If we get 1% of the market…"** — automatic flag. 1% of any market is enormous; nobody plans to capture 1% of anything.

## Distribution wedge

- **Generic GTM** — "SEO", "content marketing", "social media", "virality", "ads" without naming the specific wedge (which keyword, which sub-community, which list).
- **"We'll figure out distribution post-product"** — for ideas where distribution is the actual risk, this is a KILL trigger.
- **Two-sided marketplace with no bootstrap path** — "we'll get supply and demand to onboard at the same time" without a specific cold-start strategy.

## Defensibility / moat

- **"Unfair advantage = AI"** — AI is a tool, not a moat. Push for: proprietary data, exclusive partnership, founder credibility, regulatory licence, or switching-cost mechanism.
- **"Network effects"** without a concrete bootstrap — most products will never reach the scale where network effects matter.
- **"Great team"** as a moat — irrelevant for screening. Teams are necessary, not differentiating.
- **"First mover"** — almost never durable on its own.

## Viability

- **"We'll figure out monetisation later"** — only acceptable for genuinely consumer-distribution-first ideas. For B2B, SaaS, marketplace: KILL trigger.
- **"10x better/faster/cheaper"** — without a metric, baseline, and mechanism, this is marketing. Push for: faster from N to M minutes, cheaper from $X to $Y, better on this specific KPI.
- **"Freemium"** without a conversion mechanic — name the moment users hit the paywall and why they'll pay.

## Competitive / alternatives

- **"No direct competitors"** — almost always wrong. If literally true, ask: why has nobody built this? (often = no demand or unsolvable).
- **Misclassifying alternatives** — "Excel is not a competitor" — Excel is the most-used CRM, ERP, and project tool in the world. Always include the manual / spreadsheet / "ignore" alternative.

## Validation theatre

- **PMF claims from waitlists or signups** — neither is product-market fit.
- **Compliments counted as validation** — "people love it" coded as evidence is a red flag.
- **Survey-based pain claims** — surveys overstate pain by 3–10x. Use only as a tiebreaker, never as primary evidence.

## Regulatory / ethical

- **Regulatory shrugging in health, fintech, legal, education** — "we'll get a lawyer later" is a KILL trigger.
- **Data-economics ethics blindness** — products built on scraped personal data, copyright violations, or surveillance default-mode without consent — flag and require an ethics response.

## Process anti-patterns

- **Founder-override without naming the override** — overriding the gate is allowed; not naming it is not.
- **Composite-score worship** — a 50/55 idea with no distribution wedge is still KILL.
- **Vibes-based ITERATE** — every ITERATE verdict must name the specific dimension(s) failing AND the test that would resolve them.
