# Archetype: playful-consumer

**Feels like:** Duolingo, Figma (early), Calm, Mailchimp, Notion 2017–2019, Tumblr 2014

For: consumer products where delight and emotion drive retention. Habits, learning, creativity, communication. The product's character IS the product.

## Typography
- Display: Custom or characterful — Sharp Grotesk, GT Maru, Migra, Pangea, Söhne Breit, or hand-lettering. 700–900 weight. Sizes: 40–80px.
- Body: Friendly grotesque or rounded sans — Inter rounded, Avenir, Söhne, Söhne Schmal. 15–17px.
- Optional secondary: a script or display secondary used sparingly for emotional moments.
- Pairing rationale: a characterful display + a warm, friendly body. Type should feel like the product is talking to you, not at you.

## Color
- Background: brand-warm tones, often a tinted off-white (`#FFF9EE`, `#FFF7F0`) or a soft brand color. Dark mode is rare here; if used, it's a softened dark, not OLED black.
- Foreground: warm dark, never pure black.
- Accent: full multi-color palette — 3–5 brand colors used confidently across the product. Each color often "owns" a feature area or emotional state.
- Anti-defaults: NO grey-on-grey palettes (kills emotion). NO single-accent austerity. Embrace color.

## Motion
- Duration budget: 400–800ms with character. Motion is part of the personality.
- Easing: spring physics (`cubic-bezier(0.5, 1.5, 0.5, 1)` or actual spring with mass/tension/friction values), bouncy.
- Character: theatrical when warranted — celebratory micro-moments, character-driven idle states, mascot animations.

## Density
**Standard to generous.** Touch-friendly tap targets (48–56px). Forms feel guided, not crammed.

## Icon stance
**custom-svg** with personality, OR Phosphor Bold at large sizes, OR illustrations doing the icon job. Possibly a mascot/character system. Solid (not stroke) icons match this archetype better.

## Layout signatures
- Mascot or character system woven through the experience (Duolingo's owl)
- Celebratory animations on positive actions (level up, streak, completion)
- Emotional state in copy ("Way to go!", "Let's pick this up tomorrow")
- Color-coded sections that own emotional territory
- Big illustrations as hero, NOT hero photos
- Big rounded buttons (12–16px radius), not square
- Friendly empty states with character

## Reference sites (visit these)
1. **duolingo.com** — character system, color logic, motion character
2. **figma.com** (marketing) — color use, hero pacing, friendly seriousness balance
3. **calm.com** — emotional palette, generous whitespace, soft motion

## Anti-patterns (do NOT do these)
- Cynical or sterile tone
- Flat enterprise palette
- Over-restrained motion (kills the joy)
- Stock illustrations from Storyset / unDraw — instantly looks generic
- Icon sets with cold geometry (use rounded, soft instead)
- Tiny type
- Skeumorphic kitsch (separate from playful — playful is character, not nostalgia)
